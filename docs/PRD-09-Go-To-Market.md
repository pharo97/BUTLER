## 1. GTM Philosophy

BUTLER is a premium product for a sophisticated audience. The go-to-market approach must match the product's character:

- **Show, don't tell.** The demo video does more work than any copy.
- **Earn trust first.** Privacy is a feature. Lead with it, not bury it.
- **Community-first launch.** No paid acquisition before product-market fit is confirmed.
- **Restraint over hype.** One well-placed launch beats ten spray-and-pray campaigns.

## 3. Pre-Launch Phase (Weeks 1–12 of Engineering)

### 3.1 Waitlist Campaign
**Goal:** 2,000+ waitlist sign-ups before launch

**Tactics:**
- Landing page with:
  - Hero: 30-second silent demo GIF of Glass Chamber + pulse animation
  - Headline: *"The AI that knows when to speak — and when to stay quiet."*
  - One-line feature list (5 items max)
  - Privacy statement: "Everything stays on your Mac. Always."
  - Email capture for waitlist
  - [Watch the demo] CTA → short video
- Distribute via:
  - Twitter/X thread from founder account (personal, authentic)
  - LinkedIn post (targeting PM/executive audience)
  - Hacker News Show HN (early "what are you building" post)
  - One AI newsletter feature (negotiate free)

### 3.2 Alpha Program (Weeks 11–14 of Engineering)
- 20–50 hand-selected alpha users
- Criteria: power users who multitask heavily, willing to give detailed feedback
- Sourced from: waitlist signups, personal network, Twitter DMs
- No NDA required — encourage honest sharing
- Weekly feedback call (30 min)
- Goal: identify the top 3 usage patterns to feature in launch marketing

### 3.3 Content Pre-Production
- Demo video produced (60–90 seconds):
  - Open with someone working at their Mac — busy, tabs everywhere
  - BUTLER pulse gently appears
  - Single well-timed suggestion ("Your Downloads has 160 files…")
  - User responds, BUTLER acts
  - Cut to: voice interaction — natural, fast, no lag
  - End card: "BUTLER — beta launching [date]"
- Website copy and visuals finalized
- 5 social media posts scheduled for launch day

## 4. Launch Phase

### 4.1 Primary Launch Channel: Product Hunt
**Target:** Top 5 Product of the Day
**Launch day:** Tuesday (highest traffic)

**Pre-launch preparation:**
- Hunter identified (ideally well-known in PH community)
- First comment from founder: story, honesty, what's missing, what's next
- Assets: featured image, gallery screenshots, demo video embed
- Email waitlist 48 hours before launch: "We're launching on Product Hunt tomorrow"

**PH listing copy:**
```
BUTLER — Your AI operating companion for macOS

BUTLER lives in the corner of your screen as an animated
glass chamber. It watches what you're doing (with your permission),
and speaks up only when it actually matters.

Not another chatbot. A presence.
```

### 4.2 Secondary Channel: Hacker News "Show HN"
Post on launch day. Engineer-written. Honest about technical approach.
Example title: *"Show HN: BUTLER — a macOS AI companion with a behavioral learning layer and anti-annoyance scoring"*

Focus on: the intervention score formula, the permission architecture, the design decisions. HN audience respects technical depth.

### 4.3 Creator Partnerships
- 2–3 productivity YouTubers (100K–1M subs) given early access 2 weeks before launch
- No scripted content — genuine impressions only
- "Here's what I actually think after using BUTLER for a week"
- Target channels: MacOS-focused, productivity-focused

### 4.4 AI Newsletter Features
- Target: 2 AI newsletters to feature BUTLER in launch week
- Negotiate: product description write-up or interview format
- Target newsletters: Ben's Bites, The Rundown AI, TLDR Tech

## 5. Pricing & Conversion Strategy

### 5.1 Free Trial
- 14-day free trial, full features, no credit card required
- Day 7: in-app message: "You've been using BUTLER for 7 days. Here's what we've done together." (usage summary)
- Day 12: reminder about trial ending
- Day 14: trial ends, subscription required for continued use

### 5.2 Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price | Target |
|------|-------|--------|
| BYOK Monthly | $12/month | Technical users who already have Claude API |
| BYOK Annual | $99/year ($8.25/mo) | Committed BYOK users |
| Integrated Monthly | $35/month | Users who want simplicity, no API management |
| Integrated Annual | $299/year ($24.91/mo) | Best value for integrated tier |

### 5.3 Pricing Psychology
- Annual saves 30%+ — feature this prominently
- BYOK is the entry point — converts to Integrated over time as user trust grows
- Premium voice packs and custom skins as upsell (one-time purchases)

### 5.4 Conversion Funnel
```
Landing page → Waitlist → Free download → 14-day trial → Paid subscription
```
Target conversion rates:
- Waitlist → Download: 40%
- Download → Trial activation: 80%
- Trial → Paid: 25%

## 6. Post-Launch Retention Strategy

### 6.1 Value Delivery Loop
- Monthly usage report delivered via BUTLER: "This month, I sorted 340 files, ran 12 automations, and reminded you about 8 calendar gaps."
- Users who see concrete value don't churn.

### 6.2 Re-engagement
- If no BUTLER interaction in 5 days: gentle in-app nudge (not email)
- "You've been quiet. Ready to pick up where we left off?"

### 6.3 Feature Announcements
- New feature = BUTLER announces it conversationally
- "I've learned a new trick. Want to see?"
- Not email blasts — in-product, on-brand

### 6.4 Community Building
- Discord server: power users, feedback, feature requests
- Monthly "BUTLER Tips" thread in community
- Alpha/beta access to community members for new features

## 10. Competitive Positioning

| Product | What It Is | BUTLER Difference |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Siri | System assistant, reactive | Proactive, persistent, learnable, beautiful |
| Raycast | Keyboard launcher | BUTLER is not a launcher; it's ambient |
| Alfred | Keyboard launcher | Same; BUTLER doesn't require a keypress to be useful |
| ChatGPT Desktop | Chatbot | BUTLER is OS-aware, personality-driven, not a chat window |
| Rewind | Screen recorder + AI search | BUTLER acts and suggests; doesn't record screen |

**Core positioning statement:**
> BUTLER is the first AI operating companion that feels like a presence, not a product. It observes (with permission), learns your patterns, and speaks only when it actually matters.
